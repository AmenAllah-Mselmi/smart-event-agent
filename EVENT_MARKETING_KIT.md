## 🌟 Proposed Google Developer Experts (GDEs)

To elevate your event, inviting GDEs is a fantastic strategy. Since I don't know your specific region, I recommend searching the [GDE Directory](https://developers.google.com/community/experts) for local experts, as they often travel to local community events.

Here are top categories and examples of prominent global GDEs you could invite (or look for equivalents in your region):

**1. Machine Learning / AI GDEs:**
* *Why them:* They can lead sessions on TensorFlow, Keras, Jax, and the Gemini API.
* *Examples to look up:* **Sayak Paul** (Computer Vision & ML), **Margaret Maynard-Reid** (ML, Design), **Charmi Chokshi** (GenAI & ML).

**2. Google Cloud Platform (GCP) GDEs:**
* *Why them:* Crucial for the backend/infrastructure talks. They can speak on Kubernetes, Serverless, and deploying AI at scale.
* *Examples to look up:* **Wietse Venema** (Cloud Run & Serverless), **Antonios Chalkiopoulos** (Cloud Architecture).

**3. Web Technologies GDEs:**
* *Why them:* Perfect for frontend sessions covering Angular, Next.js, Web Performance, and Chrome features.
* *Examples to look up:* **Wassim Chegham** (Web, Angular, Cloud), **Debbie O'Brien** (Testing, Nuxt, Web).

**Pro-Tip for reaching out to GDEs:** When you contact them on LinkedIn or Twitter, highlight your expected audience size, the community impact, and mention that you'd love for them to share their specific expertise on a topic they are passionate about. Many are happy to speak at grassroots community events!
